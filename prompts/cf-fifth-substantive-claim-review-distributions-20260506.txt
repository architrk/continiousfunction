Exact claim:
"A distribution is the probability law induced by a random variable: it pushes probability from raw outcomes onto measurable value sets, with PMFs and densities as common representations."

Primary sources checked:
- Deisenroth, Faisal, and Ong, "Mathematics for Machine Learning", official free PDF from https://mml-book.github.io/
  - Chapter 6.1-6.2, Probability and Random Variables / Discrete and Continuous Probabilities.
- Goodfellow, Bengio, and Courville, "Deep Learning", official HTML book:
  - Chapter 3.3 Probability Distributions, https://www.deeplearningbook.org/contents/prob.html

Source evidence I checked:
- MML chapter 6.1.2 says random variables transfer probability from the basic probability space to a convenient target space and introduces the idea of a distribution or law associated with a random variable.
- MML defines a target-space function `X: Omega -> T` that takes an outcome and returns a quantity of interest, and calls that mapping a random variable.
- MML gives the coin example where two raw outcomes map to the same value and the probability mass function aggregates their probabilities.
- MML gives `P_X(S) = P(X in S) = P(X^-1(S)) = P({omega in Omega : X(omega) in S})`, then says `P_X`, equivalently `P o X^-1`, is the law or distribution of random variable `X`.
- MML chapter 6.2 says for discrete target spaces `P(X=x)` is the probability mass function, and for continuous target spaces it uses CDFs and PDFs/densities with interval probability given by integrating the density.
- Goodfellow chapter 3.3 says a probability distribution describes how likely a random variable or set of random variables is to take each possible state.
- Goodfellow chapter 3.3 says discrete-variable distributions may be described with PMFs, and a PMF maps a random-variable state to the probability of that state.
- Goodfellow chapter 3.3 says continuous-variable distributions are described using PDFs, and density gives probability through integration over regions rather than point probability.

Local witness alignment:
- The page states the distribution/law of `X` is `P_X(A)=P(X in A)=P({omega in Omega : X(omega) in A})`.
- The page states this is the pushforward of `P` through `X`.
- The page states the discrete PMF as `p_X(x)=P(X=x)` and, for finite raw sample spaces, sums all raw outcomes mapping to `x`.
- The page states density values are not themselves probabilities and interval probabilities are computed by integration.
- The page caveat limits this review to probability-law framing and common PMF/density vocabulary, not measure-theoretic edge cases or every distribution family used in ML.
