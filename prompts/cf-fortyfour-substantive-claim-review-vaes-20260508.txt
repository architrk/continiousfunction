Claim:
"A VAE fits a latent-variable generative model by maximizing the ELBO: q_phi(z|x) approximates the usually intractable posterior, E_q[log p_theta(x|z)] rewards reconstruction likelihood, KL(q_phi(z|x)||p(z)) penalizes deviation from the prior, and reparameterization gives pathwise gradients through z samples."

Sources:
- Kingma and Welling, 2013, "Auto-Encoding Variational Bayes"
- Rezende, Mohamed, and Wierstra, 2014, "Stochastic Backpropagation and Approximate Inference in Deep Generative Models"

Source support I verified before asking you:
- Kingma/Welling introduce `q_phi(z|x)` as a recognition model approximating the intractable true posterior `p_theta(z|x)`, and describe it as a probabilistic encoder learned jointly with the generative model.
- Kingma/Welling Eq. 1 rewrites `log p_theta(x_i)` as `KL(q_phi(z|x_i)||p_theta(z|x_i)) + L(theta,phi;x_i)`, making the nonnegative KL the gap between marginal likelihood and ELBO.
- Kingma/Welling Eq. 3 writes the ELBO as a reconstruction expected log likelihood minus `KL(q_phi(z|x_i)||p_theta(z))`, and explains that the KL term regularizes the approximate posterior toward the prior.
- Kingma/Welling Sec. 2.3-2.4 reparameterizes `z ~ q_phi(z|x)` as a differentiable transform of external noise, with the Gaussian case `z = mu + sigma epsilon`, enabling low-variance pathwise/stochastic-gradient optimization.
- Rezende et al. introduce a recognition model as an approximate posterior, optimize a lower bound on marginal likelihood for deep latent Gaussian models, decompose the free energy into a KL-to-prior regularizer plus reconstruction error, and use stochastic backpropagation for recognition/generative parameters.
