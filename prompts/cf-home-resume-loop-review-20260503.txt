Project: Continuous Function, a static-export Next.js learning atlas for deep learning, mathematics, and eventually cross-domain research learning.

Current product philosophy:
- Learners should move from a paper or question into concepts, equations, routes, labs, and discussion prompts without losing the current question.
- The site should grow slowly and rigorously: one refined route at a time, not a bulk idea dump.
- The first complete product loop is paper map -> graph route -> attention-serving KV-cache lab.
- It must be honest about what is live versus planned. Browser localStorage is acceptable for this early static-export stage.

Recent change to review:
I added a homepage resume panel that reads `cf:last-learning-route` after mount and, if present, lets a learner continue the same paper/question from home. It shows paper title, route labels, current question, carried equation, first repair, live/planned lab status, and actions to continue in graph, open the KV memory lab when live, or clear the browser-saved route.
