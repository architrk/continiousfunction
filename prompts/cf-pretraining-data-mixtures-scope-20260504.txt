Primary-source grounding I checked before asking you:
- Chinchilla / Hoffmann et al. (2022): compute-optimal training scales model size and training tokens together under a fixed recipe; this is the page we are extending, not replacing. https://arxiv.org/abs/2203.15556
- FineWeb dataset card: Common Crawl was processed, filtered, and deduplicated into about 15T GPT-2 tokens; FineWeb reports stronger benchmark performance than other public web datasets under its ablations. https://huggingface.co/datasets/HuggingFaceFW/fineweb
- FineWeb-Edu dataset card: Llama3/Phi3-style educational quality classifiers motivate filtering; threshold 3 removed 92% of FineWeb and left 1.3T educational tokens; threshold 2 retained 5.4T tokens with lower performance. https://huggingface.co/datasets/HuggingFaceFW/fineweb-edu
- Dolma official page: an open 3T-token corpus mixing web content, academic publications, code, books, and encyclopedic materials, plus a toolkit with built-in taggers and fast deduplication. https://allenai.github.io/dolma/
- DataComp-LM / DCLM NeurIPS 2024: standardized 240T-token Common Crawl corpus; benchmark lets participants test deduplication, filtering, and data mixing; model-based filtering was key to a strong public baseline. https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2024/file/19e4ea30dded58259665db375885e412-Paper-Datasets_and_Benchmarks_Track.pdf
- Data Mixing Laws / Ye et al. ICLR 2025: mixture proportions crucially affect model competence; small-scale mixture experiments can predict unseen mixture performance and guide large-run mixture selection. https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.16952
- Llama 3 Herd of Models: data mix selection used knowledge classification and scaling-law experiments; final pretraining mix roughly 50% general knowledge, 25% math/reasoning, 17% code, 8% multilingual; training adjusted mix by increasing non-English, upsampling math, adding recent web data, and downsampling lower-quality subsets. https://arxiv.org/pdf/2407.21783
