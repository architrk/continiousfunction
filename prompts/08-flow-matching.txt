Create a React component visualizing Flow Matching vs traditional Diffusion paths.

Key insight: Diffusion takes curved, noisy paths. Flow Matching learns straight optimal transport paths - fewer steps needed.

Requirements:
1. Show source distribution (noise) on left, target distribution (data) on right
2. Diffusion mode: particles take curved, wandering paths
3. Flow Matching mode: particles take straight lines (optimal transport)
4. Toggle between modes to compare
5. Show step counter: diffusion needs ~50 steps, flow matching needs ~1-4
6. Visualize the velocity field v(x,t) as arrows
7. Animate "rectification" - curved paths straightening over training

The velocity field visualization is key - show how flow matching learns simpler fields.

Colors: source in gray, target in teal (#14b8a6), paths in orange (#f59e0b).
Dark background: #080c14

Output TypeScript/TSX for Next.js with smooth animations.
