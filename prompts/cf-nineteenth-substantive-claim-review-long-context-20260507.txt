Exact claim:
"Long-context engineering has two separate constraints: positional extrapolation must keep attention usable beyond training lengths, and KV-cache memory management must keep long-context serving feasible."

Current sources:
- su-2021-roformer: Su et al. 2021, "RoFormer: Enhanced Transformer with Rotary Position Embedding"
- press-2021-alibi: Press, Smith, and Lewis 2021/ICLR 2022, "Train Short, Test Long: Attention with Linear Biases Enables Input Length Extrapolation"
- kwon-2023-pagedattention: Kwon et al. 2023, "Efficient Memory Management for Large Language Model Serving with PagedAttention"
- Current URLs in concept.yaml are arXiv abstract URLs, not direct PDFs.

Primary-source evidence I extracted:
- RoFormer abstract: RoPE encodes absolute position with a rotation matrix while incorporating explicit relative-position dependency in self-attention. It lists flexibility of sequence length and long-text classification evaluation, but it is not itself a modern RoPE-scaling recipe.
- RoFormer Section 3.1: the desired inner product should depend on word embeddings and the relative position m - n. Section 3.2 generalizes RoPE with a rotary matrix and shows the query-key product depends on the position difference through a rotation product.
- ALiBi abstract: the paper asks how a Transformer extrapolates at inference time to sequences longer than training, says changing the position-representation method can enable extrapolation, and introduces ALiBi as a linear attention-score bias instead of adding positional embeddings.
- ALiBi abstract/results: a 1.3B model trained on length 1024 extrapolates to 2048; the paper frames this as input-length extrapolation and compares against other position methods.
- ALiBi intro/figure text: current position methods degrade under longer validation input lengths, while ALiBi can train on short sequences and be applied to longer runtime sequences.
- PagedAttention abstract: high-throughput LLM serving requires batching, but systems struggle because per-request KV-cache memory is huge and grows/shrinks dynamically; inefficient management wastes memory through fragmentation and duplication, limiting batch size.
- PagedAttention Section 1/2: autoregressive generation caches key/value vectors and becomes memory-bound; batching throughput is constrained by GPU memory.
- PagedAttention Section 3: serving throughput is memory-bound because batch size is constrained by GPU memory, especially KV-cache storage. It gives an OPT-13B example where one token's KV cache is 800 KB and one request can need up to 1.6 GB.
- PagedAttention Section 4: PagedAttention stores continuous keys/values in non-contiguous blocks and vLLM manages physical KV-cache memory in a paged fashion.
