- Title: Layer Normalization & RMSNorm

Exact claim:
"LayerNorm normalizes one token/example vector across its features by subtracting that vector's mean and dividing by its standard deviation, then applying learned gain/bias. RMSNorm uses the same per-vector rescaling frame but omits mean-centering and divides by the root mean square."

Current sources:
- ba-2016-layer-normalization: Ba, Kiros, and Hinton 2016, "Layer Normalization"
- zhang-2019-rmsnorm: Zhang and Sennrich 2019, "Root Mean Square Layer Normalization"
- Current URLs in concept.yaml are arXiv abstract URLs, not direct PDFs.

Primary-source evidence I extracted:
- Ba et al. abstract: LayerNorm computes the mean and variance used for normalization from all summed inputs to neurons in a layer on a single training case. It also gives each neuron adaptive bias and gain applied after normalization and before the nonlinearity. It performs the same computation at training and test time.
- Ba et al. Section 3: LayerNorm computes statistics over all hidden units in the same layer: mu_l = (1/H) sum_i a_i^l and sigma_l = sqrt((1/H) sum_i (a_i^l - mu_l)^2). It says all hidden units in a layer share the same mu and sigma, while different training cases have different normalization terms.
- Ba et al. RNN equation: h_t = f[(g/sigma_t) odot (a_t - mu_t) + b], where b and g are bias and gain parameters with the same dimension as h_t.
- Zhang/Sennrich abstract: RMSNorm hypothesizes re-centering invariance in LayerNorm is dispensable and regularizes summed inputs according to root mean square (RMS), giving rescaling invariance.
- Zhang/Sennrich Section 3 reviews LayerNorm as a_i_bar = ((a_i - mu)/sigma) g_i, y_i = f(a_i_bar + b_i), with mu and sigma estimated from raw summed inputs.
- Zhang/Sennrich Section 4 defines RMSNorm as a_i_bar = (a_i / RMS(a)) g_i, RMS(a)=sqrt((1/n) sum_i a_i^2), and says RMSNorm simplifies LayerNorm by totally removing the mean statistic in Eq. 3.
- Zhang/Sennrich Section 4.1 gives the general RMSNorm form y = f((Wx / RMS(a)) odot g + b), and states RMSNorm is invariant to input/weight rescaling but not to re-centering operations.
