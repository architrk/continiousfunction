Context:
Oracle scoped this page as a narrow DFA mask page, not a tool-calling or JSON-product survey. I implemented a finite token-level automaton for an exact-order retrieval-call schema:

{"tool":("retrieve"|"lookup"),"source":("docs"|"tickets"),"k":(1|2)} <eos>

The page teaches:
- the model still produces ordinary next-token logits
- structured decoding carries a finite schema automaton state
- invalid next tokens are masked to probability zero and remaining valid tokens are renormalized
- allowed tokens mean tokens that can still reach an accepting state
- top-p should be applied after the hard schema mask
- the guarantee is only membership in the formal schema language, not truth, semantic correctness, safety, or correct tool use
- serving systems may pay/carry mask computation overhead during decode
