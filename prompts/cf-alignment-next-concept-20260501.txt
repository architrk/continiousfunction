Project: Continuous Function, a slow-growth mathematical learning atlas.

The current alignment branch is now published:
- RLHF: reward modeling plus KL-regularized finite-action probability shaping
- DPO: reference-relative Bradley-Terry / logistic preference objective
- KTO: binary desirable/undesirable feedback objective with prospect-style weights
- Reward Hacking: selected proxy error under KL-regularized optimization, with uncertainty penalties as brakes

The user wants slow, high-quality growth, not a flood of pages. The next concept should extend the branch naturally and be teachable with a small, honest mathematical model plus a visual/interactive demo.

- tree-search reasoning
