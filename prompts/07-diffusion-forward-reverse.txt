Create a React component visualizing the diffusion process (forward noising and reverse denoising).

Key insight: Forward process adds Gaussian noise step by step until pure noise. Reverse process learns to denoise, effectively generating data.

Requirements:
1. Start with a simple 2D distribution (e.g., two Gaussian blobs or a spiral)
2. Show particles/points representing data samples
3. Forward process: animate particles spreading into uniform noise
4. Reverse process: animate noise condensing back into the original distribution
5. Slider to control timestep t (0 = data, T = noise)
6. Show the noise schedule β_t as a small graph
7. Display the score function ∇log p(x) as vector arrows pointing toward data

Interactive: drag the timestep slider to see intermediate states.
Play/pause button for the full animation.

Colors: data points in teal (#14b8a6), noise in gray, score vectors in orange (#f59e0b).
Background: #080c14

Output TypeScript/TSX for Next.js with D3.js for particle simulation.
