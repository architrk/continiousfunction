## Your Task

Create an interactive visualization component called `AttentionGeometryDemo.tsx` that explains:

### Concept: Scaled Dot-Product Attention Geometry

**Core math to visualize:**
```
Attention(Q,K,V) = softmax(QK^T / √d_k) V
```

Where Q = XW_Q, K = XW_K, V = XW_V

**Missing intuition to address:**
- Q-K dot products measure angles between feature directions in high-dimensional space
- Softmax creates a "distribution over who to copy from" along the sequence
- The √d_k scaling prevents dot products from becoming too large (saturation)
- Multi-head attention = multiple learned "kernels" over positions/features

**Interactive elements needed:**
1. A small sequence of 4-6 tokens represented as 2D vectors (projectable to screen)
2. Show Q vectors as arrows from origin, K vectors as arrows
3. Visualize the QK^T matrix as a heatmap
4. Show how softmax transforms raw scores into attention weights
5. Slider for temperature (√d_k scaling factor) to show saturation effects
6. Toggle between "pre-softmax scores" and "post-softmax weights"

**Visualization panels:**
Panel 1: Token embeddings as points in 2D space with Q/K projections as arrows
Panel 2: QK^T attention score matrix (heatmap)
Panel 3: Post-softmax attention weights (heatmap with row sums = 1)
Panel 4: Final output showing weighted combination of V vectors

**Key insights to convey:**
- When two tokens have aligned Q-K directions, attention is high
- Softmax creates competition: attending more to one token = less to others
- Low temperature → sharper attention (nearly one-hot)
- High temperature → uniform attention (every token equally weighted)

**Educational goal:**
User should develop geometric intuition for attention as "soft dictionary lookup" where Q is the query, K are the keys, and the dot product measures relevance. They should see why scaling matters and how softmax creates a probability distribution.

Generate the complete TypeScript React component with SVG-based visualization.
