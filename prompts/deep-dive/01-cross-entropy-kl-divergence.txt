## Your Task

Create an interactive visualization component called `CrossEntropyKLDemo.tsx` that explains:

### Concept: Maximum Likelihood, Cross-Entropy & KL Divergence

**Core math to visualize:**
1. Cross-entropy: H(p, q) = -E_p[log q(x)] = -Σ p(x) log q(x)
2. KL divergence: KL(p || q) = Σ p(x) log(p(x)/q(x)) = H(p,q) - H(p)
3. Show that minimizing cross-entropy = minimizing KL divergence (up to constant)

**Missing intuition to address:**
- Why KL direction matters: KL(p_data || p_model) vs KL(p_model || p_data)
- Forward KL (mode-covering) vs Reverse KL (mode-seeking) behavior
- How cross-entropy shapes hallucination behavior under distribution shift

**Interactive elements needed:**
1. Two discrete probability distributions (p_true and q_model) shown as bar charts
2. Sliders to adjust q_model's distribution
3. Real-time display of: H(p), H(p,q), KL(p||q), KL(q||p)
4. Visual showing the "direction" of KL - which modes get covered/ignored
5. Animation showing what happens when q assigns near-zero probability to a mode p cares about

**Visualization requirements:**
- Side-by-side bar charts for p and q distributions
- Color-coded bars showing contribution to cross-entropy term
- Highlight the asymmetry: KL(p||q) ≠ KL(q||p)
- Show the "infinite penalty" when q(x)→0 but p(x)>0

**Educational goal:**
User should understand why LLMs trained with cross-entropy (forward KL) tend to "cover all modes" which can lead to hallucinations - the model would rather assign some probability to wrong answers than risk infinite loss by assigning zero.

Generate the complete TypeScript React component following the patterns shown above.
