Exact claim:
"Maximum likelihood treats observed data as fixed and fits parameters by maximizing the product of model probabilities, usually by maximizing summed log likelihood or minimizing negative log likelihood."

Primary source checked:
- Goodfellow, Bengio, and Courville, "Deep Learning", official HTML book, https://www.deeplearningbook.org/
  - Chapter 5 Machine Learning Basics, especially sections 5.5 and 5.6: https://www.deeplearningbook.org/contents/ml.html

Source evidence I checked:
- Goodfellow chapter 5.5 considers a dataset `X = {x^(1), ..., x^(m)}` drawn independently from `p_data`.
- It defines a parametric model family `p_model(x; theta)` and defines the maximum likelihood estimator as `theta_ML = arg max_theta p_model(X; theta)`.
- It then decomposes the i.i.d. likelihood into a product over examples: `arg max_theta prod_i p_model(x^(i); theta)`.
- It says the product is inconvenient, and taking logs gives an equivalent optimization problem that turns the product into a sum: `arg max_theta sum_i log p_model(x^(i); theta)`.
- It divides by `m` to express the criterion as an empirical expectation, then connects minimizing KL to minimizing `-E_data log p_model(x)`.
- It says software often phrases this as minimizing a cost function, so maximum likelihood becomes minimization of negative log-likelihood, equivalently cross-entropy.
- Chapter 5.5.1 generalizes MLE to conditional probabilities `P(y | x; theta)` and, for i.i.d. examples, decomposes conditional log-likelihood into `sum_i log P(y^(i) | x^(i); theta)`.
- Chapter 5.6 contrasts maximum likelihood as a point estimate of `theta` with Bayesian statistics, which uses a posterior distribution over parameter values.

Local witness alignment:
- The page states the data values are fixed inside the likelihood expression and the optimized variable is `theta`.
- The page states `L(theta)=prod_i p_theta(x_i)`, `ell(theta)=sum_i log p_theta(x_i)`, and average NLL `-1/n sum_i log p_theta(x_i)`.
- The Bernoulli math, code witness, and interactive demo show likelihood/NLL as functions of `theta`, with the empirical count fixed.
- The page caveat limits this review to the likelihood objective, not Bayesian posterior interpretation or a guarantee that the model family can represent the data-generating distribution.
