Source excerpts I found in extracted paper text:
- Hyvarinen abstract: models may be known only up to a multiplicative normalization constant, and the proposed method minimizes expected squared distance between the model log-density gradient and observed-data log-density gradient; the resulting formula does not require the observed-data density.
- Hyvarinen section 2: for `p(x;theta)=q(x;theta)/Z(theta)`, the data-gradient score is `nabla_x log p(x;theta)=nabla_x log q(x;theta)`, so it does not depend on `Z(theta)`.
- Hyvarinen theorem/objective: the score matching objective can be written as an expectation of `1/2 ||score||^2` plus coordinate derivatives/divergence of the model score.
- Vincent section 3: score matching learns parameters of density models with intractable partition function, calls the score the gradient of log density with respect to the data, and notes this score does not depend on troublesome `Z(theta)`.
- Vincent section 4.2: DSM defines `J_DSM = E || psi(tilde x;theta) - partial log q_sigma(tilde x | x) / partial tilde x ||^2 / 2`; for the Gaussian kernel, `partial log q_sigma(tilde x|x)/partial tilde x = (x - tilde x)/sigma^2`, which moves from corrupted `tilde x` back toward clean `x`; the DSM objective is equivalent to explicit score matching on the smoothed density.
- Song et al. abstract/introduction: the reverse-time SDE transforms prior noise back into data by removing noise and depends on the time-dependent gradient field/score of perturbed data distributions; a time-dependent neural network can estimate these scores and numerical SDE solvers can generate samples.
- Song et al. section 2.1/2.2: denoising score matching objectives train a noise-conditional score network, and DDPM-style training can be written as weighted DSM where the optimum matches perturbed-data scores.
- Song et al. section 3.2/3.3: the reverse-time SDE is `dx=[f(x,t)-g(t)^2 nabla_x log p_t(x)]dt+g(t)d wbar`; once marginal scores are known for all `t`, one can simulate it to sample from `p_0`; scores are estimated by a time-dependent score-based model.
- Song et al. section 4.3: the corresponding probability flow ODE has drift `f(x,t)-1/2 g(t)^2 nabla_x log p_t(x)` and can be replaced by the time-dependent score model.
