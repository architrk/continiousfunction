Create a React component visualizing the Edge of Stability phenomenon in neural network training.

Key insight: Theory says learning rate η must satisfy η < 2/λ_max (sharpness). But neural nets violate this - sharpness hovers AT 2/η, creating controlled instability.

Requirements:
1. 3D loss landscape visualization (can be simplified 2D contours)
2. Show the optimization trajectory as it trains
3. Plot sharpness λ_max over training steps
4. Draw the "stability threshold" line at 2/η
5. Animate: sharpness rises, hits threshold, bounces, stabilizes at edge
6. Show the "bouncing" between valley walls at high learning rates
7. Slider to adjust learning rate and see different behaviors

Three regimes to show:
- Low η: stable, slow convergence
- High η: unstable, diverges
- Edge of Stability: oscillates but converges to flat minima

Colors: trajectory in orange (#f59e0b), landscape in teal gradient, threshold in red.
Background: #080c14

Output TypeScript/TSX for Next.js with D3.js or Three.js for 3D.
