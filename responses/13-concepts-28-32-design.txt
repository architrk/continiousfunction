## #28 — Automated Circuit Discovery via Patching, Attribution, and Decomposition

**Recommendation + positioning:** *“From SAEs to scalable mechanistic explanations.”* You already have circuits (#13) and SAEs (#27); the next gap is **automating** “find the circuit” so interpretability isn’t stuck at hand-crafted case studies.

1. **Concept #28 + positioning**
   Automated Circuit Discovery (ACDC → EAP → CD-T): turning mechanistic interpretability into a *search / scoring / pruning* problem over the model’s computational graph.

2. **Math core (2–3 key equations)**

* **Activation patching as a causal intervention (edge/node ablation):**
  [
  \Delta_E ;=;\left|,L(x_{\text{clean}}\mid do(E=e_{\text{corr}})) - L(x_{\text{clean}}),\right|
  ]

* **Attribution patching (first-order/Taylor approximation):**
  [
  L(x_{\text{clean}}\mid do(E=e_{\text{corr}})) \approx L(x_{\text{clean}}) + (e_{\text{corr}}-e_{\text{clean}})^\top
  \frac{\partial}{\partial e_{\text{clean}}}L(x_{\text{clean}}\mid do(E=e_{\text{clean}}))
  ]

* **Edge scoring + pruning (circuit extraction):**
  [
  s_E ;=;|\Delta_e L|\quad\text{then keep top-}k\text{ edges}
  ]

3. **Frontier model usage**

* Used in *frontier-aligned* workflows where you need to answer: “**Which internal components are responsible for behavior X?**” at a granularity like head→head edges, MLP writes, specific positions, etc.
* 2024–2025 trend: moving from slow patching-based search (ACDC) toward **faster approximations** (EAP) and **decomposition-style methods** that aim to get circuits in seconds rather than hours (CD-T).

4. **Missing intuition to teach**

* **Activation patching is causal; attribution patching is *linearized causal***. The approximation can be *useful* even when it’s not faithful pointwise—students need a strong feel for when gradients lie.
* **Metric choice is everything:** some metrics create degenerate gradients (e.g., “zero-gradient at the optimum” pathologies); automated methods inherit these failure modes.
* **Circuits are not unique**: many sparse subgraphs can reproduce behavior; pruning reveals *a* mechanism, not *the* mechanism.

5. **2–3 canonical papers**

* Conmy et al., *Towards Automated Circuit Discovery for Mechanistic Interpretability* (NeurIPS 2023, ACDC).
* Syed et al., *Attribution Patching Outperforms Automated Circuit Discovery* (BlackBoxNLP/ACL Anthology 2024; Edge Attribution Patching framing).
* Hsu et al., *Efficient Automated Circuit Discovery in Transformers using Contextual Decomposition (CD‑T)* (ICLR 2025).

6. **Visualization ideas**

* **Circuit graph UI:** nodes = heads/MLPs at positions, edges colored by (s_E); slider for pruning threshold; show task metric vs %edges kept.
* **Patch map heatmap:** layer×head matrix of (\Delta) scores; click into a head to see its strongest incoming/outgoing edges.
* **Approximation gap demo:** plot activation-patching (\Delta_E) vs attribution score for same edges; show where linearization over/under-estimates.

7. **Prerequisites**

* #2 Attention & transformer layers
* #13 Transformer circuits & mechanistic interpretability
* #1 Cross-entropy / KL basics (for metrics)
* #27 SAEs (optional but highly synergistic)

8. **Unlocks what**

* Scaling mechanistic interpretability from “one-off archaeology” → **repeatable pipeline**.
* Enables **target selection** for steering (#29), safety interventions, and debugging.
* Bridges to “automatic red-teaming of internals” and interpretability evaluations at scale.

## #29 — Activation Steering and Feature-Guided Interventions

**Recommendation + positioning:** *“Interpretability becomes actionable.”* After SAEs (#27) and automated circuit discovery (#28), the natural next concept is **intervening** on internals to control outputs—both to test hypotheses and to get practical, inference-time control.

1. **Concept #29 + positioning**
   Activation Steering / Activation Engineering: methods that *edit hidden states* (or SAE latents) to change behavior without retraining, turning interpretability into **control knobs**.

2. **Math core (2–3 key equations)**

* **Classic steering vector addition (one layer/position):**
  [
  h_{\ell,t}^{,\text{steer}} ;=; h_{\ell,t} + \alpha,v_{\ell,t}
  ]
* **Contrastive steering vector (difference of means):**
  [
  v_{\ell,t} ;=; \mathbb{E}[h_{\ell,t}\mid \text{desired}] ;-; \mathbb{E}[h_{\ell,t}\mid \text{undesired}]
  ]
  (This is the core idea behind “instruction vectors” and many CAA-style methods.)
* **SAE-latent steering (feature toggle → decode back):**
  [
  z = f_{\text{enc}}(h_{\ell,t}),\qquad
  h_{\ell,t}^{,\text{steer}} = f_{\text{dec}}!\left(z + \delta,e_k\right)
  ]
  (With (e_k) a basis vector selecting feature (k).)

3. **Frontier model usage**

* **Inference-time control**: enforce format/length/constraints, style shifts, reduce/induce refusals, change factuality/hedging, etc., *without fine-tuning*.
* **Interpretability probe:** steering is a causal test—if adding feature (k) induces behavior, you’ve localized part of the mechanism (and can validate circuits).
* **Minimal interventions trend:** “activation scaling” learns sparse scalars to strengthen/weaken existing directions, aiming for more interpretable, less invasive edits.

4. **Missing intuition to teach**

* Steering is **geometry on the model’s manifold**: small (\alpha) nudges within-distribution; large (\alpha) can throw you off-manifold → incoherence and capability loss.
* “Interpretable steering” isn’t just *what direction*—it’s *which basis*: SAE features give humans handles, but the decoder can entangle effects unless you constrain feature selection.
* **Compositionality isn’t guaranteed**: adding two “instruction vectors” can cancel or amplify depending on where they write in residual space.

5. **2–3 canonical papers**

* Stoehr et al., *Activation Scaling for Steering and Interpreting Language Models* (EMNLP Findings 2024).
* Stolfo et al., *Improving Instruction-Following in Language Models through Activation Steering* (ICLR 2025 submission listed on arXiv).
* Soo et al., *Feature Guided Activation Additions (FGAA)* (OpenReview, 2025).

6. **Visualization ideas**

* **Logit lens delta:** show top-10 logits before/after steering; animate as (\alpha) changes.
* **SAE feature dashboard:** bars for (z_k) activations; user toggles features; show generated text changes + which features were used.
* **Steering phase diagram:** heatmap of “task success” vs (\alpha) and layer index (\ell); highlight the “safe” band before coherence collapses.

7. **Prerequisites**

* #2 Transformers + residual stream mental model
* #10 Embedding geometry
* #27 SAEs (strongly recommended)
* #28 (optional) for causal framing of interventions

8. **Unlocks what**

* Practical, modular **behavior control** without retraining.
* A causal toolkit to validate interpretability claims (“this feature causes that behavior”).
* A bridge to safety: controllable refusal/style/format constraints as *post-training knobs*.

## #30 — Long Context Engineering: RoPE Scaling + KV Memory Compression

**Recommendation + positioning:** *“How frontier models reach 128K → 1M+ tokens.”* You already cover RoPE basics (#18) and KV cache/FlashAttention (#19). The missing piece is **how we break the pretraining-length ceiling** and then **make the KV memory cost survivable**.

1. **Concept #30 + positioning**
   Long-context extension is a *two-front war*:

* **Position extrapolation** (RoPE scaling / interpolation / non-uniform rescaling), and
* **Inference memory** (KV cache quantization/compression), which dominates at long contexts.

2. **Math core (2–3 key equations)**

* **RoPE injection (complex form, key idea: relative phase):**
  [
  q_{m,[2j:2j+1]} = W_q x_m, e^{i m\theta_j},\quad
  k_{n,[2j:2j+1]} = W_k x_n, e^{i n\theta_j},\quad
  \theta_j=b^{-2j/d}
  ]

* **YaRN “NTK-by-parts” wavelength scaling (one way to extend):**
  [
  \hat{\lambda}_j = (1-\gamma_j),s\lambda_j + \gamma_j,\lambda_j,
  \quad
  \gamma_j=
  \begin{cases}
  1 & \lambda_j<L/\beta\
  0 & \lambda_j>L/\alpha\
  \frac{L/\lambda_j-\alpha}{\beta-\alpha} & \text{otherwise}
  \end{cases}
  ]

* **Attention with compressed KV (generic form):**
  [
  \text{Attn}(q,K,V)=\text{softmax}!\left(\frac{qK^\top}{\sqrt d}\right)V
  ;\approx;
  \text{Attn}(q,;C(K),;C(V))
  ]
  (Where (C(\cdot)) may prune tokens, cluster, low-rank project, or quantize.)

3. **Frontier model usage**

* **RoPE scaling methods** are a dominant route to extending transformer context windows without architectural change (e.g., YaRN and successors).
* **Extreme context via rescaling:** LongRoPE reports extending to **millions of tokens** using non-uniform positional interpolation/rescaling; it’s also presented as practical enough to integrate into an existing stack.
* **KV cache becomes the bottleneck** at very long contexts; KVQuant specifically targets KV activation quantization and reports enabling **~1M context on a single A100‑80GB** (and much longer with more GPUs) at low perplexity degradation.

4. **Missing intuition to teach**

* The failure mode isn’t “the model forgets”—it’s often **phase/angle OOD**: RoPE dimensions have wavelengths, and beyond training range you hit unseen rotations (“critical dimensions” framing).
* Long context breaks attention in two ways:
  **(a)** position encoding extrapolation, and **(b)** **attention entropy / softmax temperature drift** as (T) grows (why some methods rescale attention).
* **Memory math becomes destiny**: for long contexts, KV cache storage and bandwidth dominate; quantization/compression is not optional at scale.

5. **2–3 canonical papers**

* Peng et al., *YaRN: Efficient Context Window Extension of Large Language Models* (ICLR 2024).
* Ding et al., *LongRoPE: Extending LLM Context Window Beyond 2 Million Tokens* (ICML 2024).
* Hooper et al., *KVQuant: Towards 10 Million Context Length LLM Inference with KV Cache Quantization* (2024).

6. **Visualization ideas**

* **RoPE angle plot:** show (m\theta_j) vs position (m) for several (j); overlay scaled variants to make “OOD angle ranges” obvious.
* **KV memory budget chart:** memory = layers × heads × seq_len × head_dim × bytes; compare FP16 vs 4-bit vs 3-bit (interactive slider).
* **Attention entropy vs length:** plot entropy for baseline vs attention-scaled variant; annotate “why logits scaling helps.”

7. **Prerequisites**

* #18 RoPE
* #19 KV cache, GQA & FlashAttention
* #21 Serving (prefill/decode) for why KV dominates
* #2 Attention basics

8. **Unlocks what**

* The playbook behind modern 32k/128k/256k (and beyond) context windows.
* Ability to reason about long-context tradeoffs: “positional method vs KV compression vs architecture change.”
* Sets up the motivation for #31 (SSMs/hybrids as KV-free/linear-time alternatives).

## #31 — State Space Models and Hybrid Architectures: Mamba‑2 / SSD / Griffin / Jamba

**Recommendation + positioning:** *“Architectures that scale in time and memory.”* Long context (#30) exposes the transformer’s Achilles heel (quadratic attention + ever-growing KV cache). The clean conceptual next step is **linear-time sequence modeling** and **hybrids** that retain transformer strengths.

1. **Concept #31 + positioning**
   State Space Models (SSMs) + hybrids: replace global attention with **recurrences / structured kernels**, or mix both (often with local attention + recurrence) for long-context efficiency.

2. **Math core (2–3 key equations)**

* **SSM recurrence:**
  [
  h_{t+1}=Ah_t + Bx_t,\qquad y_t=Ch_t
  ]
* **Equivalent convolution/kernel view:**
  [
  y_t=\sum_{k=0}^{t}K_k x_{t-k},\qquad K_k = CA^{k}B
  ]
* **Hybrid gating intuition (one generic template):**
  [
  y_t = g_t\odot y_t^{\text{SSM}} + (1-g_t)\odot y_t^{\text{Attn(local)}}
  ]
  (Captures Griffin-style “recurrence + local attention” hybrids at a conceptual level.)

3. **Frontier model usage**

* **Mamba‑2 / SSD:** Dao & Gu (2024) explicitly frame *Structured State-Space Duality* connecting SSMs and attention and introduce Mamba‑2, described as faster while staying competitive.
* **Jamba:** hybrid **Transformer–Mamba** architecture, interleaving blocks and adding MoE for capacity; reported strong long-context performance up to 256K tokens in open releases.
* **RecurrentGemma / Griffin:** mixes **linear recurrences with local attention**, aiming for efficiency and long-sequence suitability.

4. **Missing intuition to teach**

* SSMs can be taught as “**attention with a structured kernel**”: both compute weighted sums over the past, but SSMs do it via a recurrence/scan that can be **hardware-friendly and linear-time**.
* The reason “SSMs work for language now” is **selectivity** (input-dependent behavior), not just (O(T)) complexity—otherwise you get a bland smoothing kernel that can’t do sharp retrieval.
* Hybrids exist because you often want:
  **local attention** for short-range syntax + **recurrence/SSM** for long-range memory.

5. **2–3 canonical papers**

* Dao & Gu, *Transformers are SSMs: … Structured State Space Duality (Mamba‑2)* (ICML 2024 / arXiv 2024).
* *Jamba: Hybrid Transformer‑Mamba Language Models* (ICLR 2025 conference paper; arXiv 2024).
* *RecurrentGemma: Moving Past Transformers for Efficient Open Language Models* (2024 technical report).

6. **Visualization ideas**

* **Scaling plot:** attention compute/memory (O(T^2)) and KV growth (O(T)) vs SSM compute (O(T)) and constant state memory.
* **“Receptive field” animation:** show how a recurrence updates state each step; contrast with attention “look-back” matrix.
* **Hybrid block diagram:** interleave Transformer blocks and SSM blocks; show where MoE plugs in (connect to #22/#23).

7. **Prerequisites**

* #2 Attention & transformers
* #19 Efficient attention at scale
* #22 Sparse MoE (for Jamba-style hybrids)
* #30 Long context engineering (motivates the shift)

8. **Unlocks what**

* Understanding why “post-transformer” doesn’t mean “no transformer” (hybrids dominate).
* A principled way to reason about **long-context cost** and why some models avoid KV caches.
* Architectural vocabulary for frontier trends beyond vanilla Transformers.

## #32 — Multimodal Foundations: Vision Encoders, Contrastive Pretraining, and Cross-Attention Fusion

**Recommendation + positioning:** *“Start the multimodal arc cleanly.”* You already cover diffusion (#9) and representation learning (#10), but you’re missing the bridge: **how images become tokens** and how models learn **shared semantic spaces** that feed multimodal LLMs and diffusion conditioning.

1. **Concept #32 + positioning**
   Vision-Language Pretraining (VLP): train **image encoder + text encoder** (or text-free variants) so that images and text land in a shared space; then fuse into a generative model (often via cross-attention).

2. **Math core (2–3 key equations)**

* **Dual-encoder similarity (normalized embeddings):**
  [
  u_i=\frac{f_I(I_i)}{|f_I(I_i)|},\quad v_j=\frac{f_T(T_j)}{|f_T(T_j)|},\quad
  s_{ij}=\frac{u_i^\top v_j}{\tau}
  ]

* **CLIP-style contrastive loss (InfoNCE / symmetric cross-entropy):**
  [
  \mathcal{L}=\frac12\left(
  -\log\frac{e^{s_{ii}}}{\sum_j e^{s_{ij}}}
  -\log\frac{e^{s_{ii}}}{\sum_j e^{s_{ji}}}
  \right)
  ]

* **Cross-attention fusion (text attends to vision tokens):**
  [
  \text{CrossAttn}(H_T,H_I)=\text{softmax}!\left(\frac{(H_TW_Q)(H_IW_K)^\top}{\sqrt d}\right)(H_IW_V)
  ]

3. **Frontier model usage**

* **Modern VLP recipes:** SigLIP 2 (2025) describes a unified training recipe extending the image-text objective with captioning-based pretraining, self-supervised losses, and online curation, producing better encoders for downstream VLMs.
* **Localization-aware contrastive:** CLOC (2024) explicitly adds region-level objectives to improve localization/dense features, motivated by needs of multimodal LLMs (region understanding).
* These encoders become the **front-end** for multimodal LLMs, and the **conditioning backbone** for text-to-image systems (e.g., diffusion uses text embeddings/attention conditioning—your #9 becomes more complete once this is in place).

4. **Missing intuition to teach**

* **Contrastive ≠ dense understanding**: CLIP-like training often yields global semantics; localization and dense features require extra losses/architecture.
* **Negatives and batch size matter**: contrastive training is heavily shaped by the similarity matrix; students should “see” how diagonal dominance emerges.
* **Fusion choice is capability choice**: dual-encoder retrieval is cheap; cross-attention fusion enables richer grounding but costs compute and needs careful tokenization (patches/regions).

5. **2–3 canonical papers**

* Radford et al., *Learning Transferable Visual Models From Natural Language Supervision (CLIP)* (2021) — baseline paradigm.
* Tschannen et al., *SigLIP 2: Multilingual Vision-Language Encoders…* (2025).
* Chen et al., *Contrastive Localized Language-Image Pre-Training (CLOC)* (2024).

6. **Visualization ideas**

* **Similarity matrix heatmap:** batch image×text scores (s_{ij}); show how training sharpens the diagonal over time.
* **Retrieval demo:** input image → top-k captions; input caption → top-k images; show embedding distances live.
* **Cross-attention maps:** for a caption token (“red”, “dog”), visualize attention over image patches/regions; compare CLIP vs CLOC-like localized features.

7. **Prerequisites**

* #2 Attention & transformers
* #10 Embedding geometry
* #1 Cross-entropy / softmax
* (Optional) #9 Diffusion (to connect how text embeddings condition generative vision)

8. **Unlocks what**

* Full conceptual bridge to **multimodal LLMs** (how vision becomes tokens + how grounding works).
* Better explanation of text-conditioned diffusion and future video models.
* Foundations for later concepts like multimodal RLHF, grounding evals, and VLM safety/robustness.
